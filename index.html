Machine Learning Researcher | 2nd year Ph.D. student at UC San Diego
I am a second-year PhD student, working as a Graduate Student Researcher at the University of California San Diego. Broadly, my interests lie in Time-Series Data, Sensor Fusion Network and Applied Machine Learning. I work under the joint supervision of Prof. Rajesh K. Gupta at the Microelectronic Embedded Systems Laboratory (MESL) and Prof. Jingbo Shang at the Data Mining Laboratory, studying the robustness and efficiency of learning algorithm in time-series data. My current research projects are funded and supported by the National Science Foundation (NSF) and Computing On Network Infrastructure for Pervasive Perception, Cognition and Action (CONIX). I am one of the 10 recipients of Halıcıoğlu Data Science Institute (HDSI) Graduate Prize Fellowship, provided to support my data-science related research at UC San Diego.
In my previous life, I was a Research Assistant in Computer Science and Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology, having completed my B.Sc. from the same department. I received several honors for my undergraduate studies including University Merit Scholarship, Dean's List and invited young researcher at The Cornell, Maryland, Max Planck Pre-doctoral Research School (CMMRS) 2019 and Heidelberg Laureate Forum (HLF) 2020.

Software Development Engineer Intern
As a Software Development Engineer with an expertise in Machine Learning (ML), I built an ML Model Introspection and Explanation Framework from the SQL surface for Amazon Redshift. This will benefit database users and data analysts, familiar with SQL but with little or no prior experience in ML and its related framework, to leverage ML services by writing SQL queries. Loaded data from database tables in Redshift and used trained Neo-compiled model from S3 to compute and store SHAP Feature Importance values. This improved the execution speed of the new workflow by 2x and reduced its memory footprint by 90% over the existing workflow that uses Amazon Sagemaker.

Research Experience

University of California San Diego

Graduate Student Researcher
  • A universal multitask Transformer model to learn a general representation of time-series data that improves classification and forecasting performance. We design a masked data modelling technique to enable self-supervised training of Transformers by harnessing the auto-regressive nature of time-series data. [In progress]

  • A Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) based Super-Resolution model to generate high-resolution spatiotemporal data from low-resolution and irregularly-spaced sparse sensory data. We use a UNet architecture with 3-D partial convolution as a Local Completion Network paired with 3-D self-attention as a Global Completion Network to train a parallel-branched multiresolution generator and discriminator. [Under submission]

  • A comprehensive evaluation strategy and a model-agnostic training framework to facilitate robustness of machine learning models to missing feature sets in time-series data. We achieved a 22% improvement in robustness over the baseline for a bunch of models across benchmark classification tasks in sensor fusion networks. [Under submission]
Tech Stack: Python, Pytorch, Keras
September 2019 - Present

Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology

Research Assistant
  • Innovated a novel algorithm, Real Time Principal Component Analysis (Real Time PCA), for dimensionality reduction on streaming data. The algorithm learns a robust, noise-resilient eigenspace representation of streaming data using a simple, fast heuristic. The efficacy of the algorithm was proven experimentally by spectral diagrams and statistical similarity of over 0.90.
    The short paper of the work was published at The 35th IEEE International Conference on Data Engineering (ICDE), April 2019, and its longer version was accepted for publication at the ACM Transactions on Data Science (TDS), Volume 1, Issue 2, July 2020.

  • Built and deployed a web application, based on Real Time PCA, to generate real-time analytics on streaming data fetched from Twitter, Google Search Trends, Bangladeshi Online News Portals and Dhaka Stock Exchange. The project was financed and is currently operated by the Information and Communication Technology Division of the Government of Bangladesh.
